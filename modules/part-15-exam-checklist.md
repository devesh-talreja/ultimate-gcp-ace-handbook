# Part 15 — Final Checklist Before Exam Day

## 15.1 One Week Before

- Take at least one full-length, timed practice exam under real conditions (no notes, 120-minute clock).
- Re-read Part 9 (Confusing Concepts) and Part 11 (Common Mistakes) fully.
- Redo any hands-on lab you found shaky — muscle memory beats re-reading.

## 15.2 Day Before

- Skim Part 10 (High-Yield Revision Notes) top to bottom.
- Re-read Part 6 (Rosetta Stone) once, fast — don't try to memorize new material.
- Confirm exam logistics: ID ready, OnVUE system check done (if remote), quiet room/clear desk, or test center address/time confirmed.
- Sleep well — seriously, scenario-reasoning exams punish fatigue more than fact-recall exams do.

## 15.3 During the Exam

- Read each scenario for the constraint keywords (“fewest steps,” “least privilege,” “Google recommended practice,” “global,” “no downloaded keys”) — they usually point directly at the intended answer pattern from Part 8/11.
- Flag and skip anything you're unsure of on first pass — come back with fresh eyes; don't burn your 120 minutes stuck on one question.
- For multi-select questions, re-read the question stem for “select two/three” — easy to lose an easy mark by under- or over-selecting.
- Trust your prepared decision frameworks (Part 3.1's compute tree, Part 3.5's data matrix, Part 6's Rosetta Stone) over a gut AWS-instinct reaction.
> [!TIP]
> **Exam Tip:** You've got this. Between your AWS SAA foundation and this handbook's GCP-specific depth, you're better prepared than most first-time ACE candidates. Good luck — and once you pass, come back to Part 14 and start building.
